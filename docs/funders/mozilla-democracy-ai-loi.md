### The Problem in One Paragraph

4.1 million Americans sail. 1,300 yacht clubs serve them — most as 501(c)(7) nonprofits, most with zero dedicated data staff, most losing the talent pipeline war because parents can't find basic information online: what it costs, what ages are welcome, whether their kid needs to know how to swim. Meanwhile, these same clubs sit on enormous public-interest data (IRS Form 990 filings, US Sailing participation records, federally-subsidized waterfront access rights) that no one has made useful, searchable, or AI-legible. The information gap is not a content problem. It's an infrastructure problem. And it's replicable across every civic membership sector — swim teams, hiking clubs, volunteer fire departments, community orchestras — wherever civic infrastructure is quietly maintained by underpaid staff and invisible volunteer labor.

### What We're Building

**Full Harbor** is a shared AI infrastructure practice for civic membership organizations. Our thesis: the same 20 parent questions get asked at every youth sailing program in America. Every club answers them separately, badly, and usually only to people who already know to ask. We build the answer once and deploy it everywhere.

**Product Stack:**

| Product | Function | Access |
|---|---|---|
| **Ask a Sailor** | RAG AI agent answering parent questions about youth sailing programs | Public, free |
| **Harbor Commons** | Open platform for IRS 990 nonprofit data: financial benchmarks, compensation transparency, GEO/AIO readiness scores | Public, open data |
| **Club Auditor** | Automated "parent experience audit" — grades any club website on how well it answers parent questions | Public, free |
| **Club Steward** | Internal AI intelligence agent for club boards and administrators | Private, subscription |

**What is "GEO/AIO readiness"?**
Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) / AI Overview (AIO) optimization is the emerging discipline of structuring web content so that AI language models retrieve it correctly in response to natural language queries. A club that publishes "Opti Camp: Ages 7-13, $740 members / $1,000 non-members, June 8-12" is GEO-ready. A club whose pricing is hidden behind a "Coming Soon" front door — while the real registration page exists 3 clicks deeper — is invisible to both Google's AI Overviews and ChatGPT. Full Harbor's Club Auditor scores every club website on this dimension. Harbor Commons tracks GEO readiness scores across the sector.

### The Democracy × AI Connection

Mozilla's Democracy × AI Incubator focuses on the intersection of AI and civic participation. Our work sits directly at that intersection:

**1. The information gap is a civic participation gap.**
When a non-sailing parent can't find the price of sailing camp, their child doesn't try sailing. The demographic consequence is visible in the data: sailing is 78% white, skews upper-income, and has been losing junior participant numbers for a decade. The information hiding isn't malicious — it's structural. But AI-legible open data infrastructure is the fix.

**2. We're building with, not on top of, community organizations.**
Full Harbor operates as a fractional member of host organizations (yacht clubs, sailing associations, US Sailing). We don't extract data — we return intelligence. Harbor Commons is governed by the organizations whose data it holds. Club Steward is only available to the club whose data it processes.

**3. The model generalizes.**
The Full Harbor pattern — open data layer + AI retrieval layer + community-facing agent — applies anywhere civic infrastructure is maintained by nonprofits with limited data capacity. Once built for sailing, the architecture deploys to rowing clubs, community pools, youth hockey associations, volunteer fire departments. The same infrastructure problem. The same solution.

**4. We're building open source, by design.**
The Ask a Sailor agent, the Club Auditor, and the Harbor Commons data pipeline are all MIT-licensed. A swim club in Detroit should be able to deploy Ask a Swimmer using the same codebase. A community orchestra should be able to audit its website using the same parent-question framework. Full Harbor builds the core; the commons inherits the benefit.

### Why Now

**The AI search transition is happening faster than civic organizations can adapt.**

SparkToro/Datos data from Q4 2025 shows Google at 73.7% of desktop search activity, with all AI tools combined at ~3.2%. But those numbers are converging rapidly, and more importantly: Google now generates AI Overviews for ~16% of queries. The source it uses is the same source ChatGPT uses: structured, crawlable, semantically clear web content.

A sailing club that can't answer "how much does camp cost?" on its website is invisible to Google today. It will be invisible to every AI-mediated search interface tomorrow. The organizations that can least afford a data team are the ones most harmed by this transition.

Full Harbor's timing is: build the infrastructure now, before the AI transition fully displaces traditional search, so civic organizations don't have to catch up.

### The Quiet Yield Frame

Full Harbor's foundational research concept is **Quiet Yield**: the invisible labor that makes civic membership organizations function — race officers, volunteer treasurers, youth sailing coordinators who work for club-culture wages, newsletter editors who donate their weekends.

This labor is real. It has market value. Bureau of Labor Statistics data suggests that a volunteer racing committee delivering 40 racing nights per year is providing the equivalent of ~$120,000 in professional Event Coordinator labor that never appears on any balance sheet.

When clubs can't fund data infrastructure, quiet yield expands to fill the gap — until the person absorbing it leaves or burns out. Full Harbor's shared infrastructure model replaces quiet yield labor (one volunteer at each of 1,300 clubs, separately, badly) with professional infrastructure (built once, maintained centrally, deployed everywhere).

This is Mozilla's mission applied to civic life: open, responsible technology that increases participation and reduces structural barriers.
